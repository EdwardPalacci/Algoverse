The above results were found by using a Simple Random Sample (SRS) on all the questions processed. 

The 7 models used were, for clarity, ---

	for the 4 DLM models.

We pooled  the 2250 * 7 = 15750 total questions and found a random sample of 200 questions using the random module in python. 

We chose a manual grading amount of 200 questions so we could achieve a margin of error (MOE) of at least 6.89% while keeping manual grading feasible. 

Math for the MOE (of a population proportion) : 

	Setting z-score = 1.96 to align with the scientific standard of a 95% confidence interval, then 

	MOE = z * sqrt (p(1-p)/n) * sqrt ( (N-n) / (N-1)

	where p = the proportion of correctly aligned LLM answers (taken as .5 prior to experiment),
 	n = the sample size,
	N = the total population

Afterwards, we can recalculate the MOE using the population proportion actually found, which was p = 0.985, giving a MOE = 1.67%

	MOE = (1.96) * sqrt (.985(1-.985)/200) * sqrt ((15760-200)/(15760-1)) = 0.01673960812

Furthermore,we used Cohen's kappa (the gold standard for inter-rate reliability tests) to isolate the likelihood of chance in agreenment. 

We found that between our LLM-as-judge (anthropic/claude-sonnet-4.5) and a human grader, kappa = .9688.  

In industry, a Cohen's kappa score of >.8 is treated as amazing
